### Seven habits of calm Git users

<div class="pop grid grid-cols-1 gap-y-3 mt-8 max-w-3xl mx-auto">
  <div v-click="1" class="step-row items-center"><span class="step-num">1</span><span>Pull before you start. Push when you pause.</span></div>
  <div v-click="2" class="step-row items-center"><span class="step-num">2</span><span>Branch for every change, never commit to main.</span></div>
  <div v-click="3" class="step-row items-center"><span class="step-num">3</span><span>Commit small, commit often, with clear messages.</span></div>
  <div v-click="4" class="step-row items-center"><span class="step-num">4</span><span>Run <code>git status</code> whenever you're unsure.</span></div>
  <div v-click="5" class="step-row items-center"><span class="step-num">5</span><span>Conflicts are normal; read them, don't fear them.</span></div>
  <div v-click="6" class="step-row items-center"><span class="step-num">6</span><span>Review through Merge Requests, not private merges.</span></div>
  <div v-click="7" class="step-row items-center"><span class="step-num">7</span><span>When it's messy, STOP and ask before piling on.</span></div>
</div>
